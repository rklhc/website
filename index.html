    • 01PURPOSE

      “Access to Justice”

    • 02 OBJECT

      The main object of the “ Access to Justice ” is feel good by the people of India with regard to their physical, mental,socio-economic and legal health.Lot of measures have already been taken and further steps are being taken for strengthening the physical,mental and socio-economic health of people of this country ,but nothing tangible has been done towards the legal health corner except providing some financial assistance to economically backward people of India by way of legal aid as enshrined in the Constitution of India and the process of getting the said benefit is somewhat cumbersome.Voices are coming to bring gradual reforms to reduce the huge backlog of pending cases in Indian Courts subordinate as well as higher and number of increasing Judges with required proper infrastructure can be only a short-time measure which may cause heavy exchequer on Government. But the steps for reducing the filing of cases in Courts may be a permanent solution by way of settlement through specialist and legal health awareness compaign by creating National,State and Regional Legal Health Care Centre all over the country for the poor,downtroden and old aged people of India particularly the poor women,child and senior citizens of India who are the biggest sufferer in legal health care.

    • 03 REASONS FOR PROPOSAL

      Our founder Mr. R.K.Srivastava is a District & Sessions Judge (Retd.) having distinguished and impeccable track record spanning over 40 years as an eminent Judge in Bihar Judicial Services, Government of Bihar. Mr. Srivastava, subsequently, was also appointed to the prestigious post of Lokpal (Insurance), Government of India for the states of Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh. During his long service in the state of Bihar, Mr. Srivastava realised that the poor and marginalized people, particularly the women, children and senior citizens of weaker section of society face immense difficulties in settling claims and enforcing their rights in Courts of law due to procedural complexity and delay which can be easily simplified and settled in legal health care centres. Our mission is to create legal awareness, render quality legal advise, suggest and assist in summary and alternative remedies to reduce the mounting arrear of pending cases, provide documentation services as well as litigation support, if required in filing and conduction of cases with the vision to provide legal health care to poor, downtrodden, needy and marginalized sections of the society. As a matter of concern, weaker section in India are neither conscious of their rights nor they have required resources for resorting to the appropriate remedy in case of infringement of their personal, property or constitutional rights. The victims of offences have to keep waiting for justice for the reasons of their socio-economic backwardness and for want of proper legal health care as well as legal health awareness about their juris right. The above category of people can be legally termed as “Juris Care Victims” and for the protection and welfare of legal health of juris care victims, legal health care centres can be established in different State capitals and also at district level in phase wised manner to provide special facilities to the BPL and APL families, women, children, senior citizens, ex-servicemen, disabled persons and victims of terrorist activities in this regard. The legal health care centres constituted for the purpose will also help the parties to settle theie dispute in quick and amicable manner without any heavy economic burden. The centres will also make the society at large aware about their rights by way of legal health awareness campaigns by creating National rural legal health mission to spread the object of the legal health from village to village. It is high time to make legal access affordable and easily accessible for the poor, marginalized and needy sections of the Country in order to eradicate the existing inequality amongst the Citizens in approaching and knocking the doors of justice. Mr. Srivastava, having extensive experience in the subject, has developed this organization to solve the concern of mounting arrears of court cases and bring down the litigation cost and ensure the implementation of constitutional mandate of equality before the law and also to encourage arbitration and alternative dispute resolution mechanism to ensure quicker justice out of courts. The fundamental and legal rights of citizens could become meaningful only when they are justiciable.
